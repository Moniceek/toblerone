The Matterhorn is a mountain of the Alps, straddling the main watershed and border between Switzerland and Italy. It is a large, near-symmetric pyramidal peak in the Pennine Alps, whose summit is 4,478 meters high. It makes it one of the highest summits in the Alps and Europe. The resulting pyramid-shaped peak has four distinct sides that align with the cardinal directions: north, south, east and west.
